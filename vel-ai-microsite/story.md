# Story — "The Missed Call"

Chosen concept (of three pitched):

1. **The Missed Call** — Kinetic Type — RECOMMENDED, BUILT. One phone call, lost and
   then won back. Signature moment: *scroll answers the call* — a held beat where the
   visitor's thumb picks up a ringing phone and the AI's transcript types itself in
   as they scroll.
2. **After Hours** — Graphic Editorial. A night-shift magazine: the business that
   works at 2am, flat neon cutouts, clock hands spun by scroll. Signature: the
   spinning night clock.
3. **The Second Employee** — Cinematic Film (Lane B). An empty office coming alive
   shot by shot. Not chosen: no video engine available in this environment.

## After the film (static, conventional)

- Three services, one line each: AI Receptionist / Workflow Automation / One Dashboard.
- Trust strip: live in 24 hours · no code · support in under 2 hours · solo → enterprise.
- Final CTA block (same single CTA), link to vel-ai.com.au.
- Footer: independent concept redesign disclaimer.

## Copy rules applied

- One CTA only ("Get my time back"), repeated in finale, after-film, and the fixed bar.
- No invented stats: the notification stack is a visual, not a number; the only figure
  (20+ hours/week) is the brand's own published customer claim, attributed as such.
- Every chapter ends unresolved until the finale: the hook ends on a lost call, the
  pain ends on identity ("the whole business"), the fear ends on the competitor.
